Prateek Bansal

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), specializing in computational chemistry. My research focuses on molecular modeling, simulations, and the application of machine learning techniques to solve complex biochemical problems.

I am passionate about education and outreach, having served as the Head Lab Assistant for the CURIE Summer Camp at UIUC.

Feel free to reach out to me via email at pdb3@illinois.edu or connect with me on Google Scholar and LinkedIn.

Education

I am currently pursuing my PhD in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at UIUC, where I have maintained a CGPA of 3.7/4.00. I received my Bachelor of Engineering in Chemical Engineering from the Institute of Chemical Technology in Mumbai, India, graduating with a CGPA of 9.23/10.

Technical Skills

  • Programming: Python (Expert), Machine Learning (Intermediate, with experience in scikit-learn and PyTorch), Command Line Interface, Git and GitHub
  • Molecular Modeling: Autodock, RosettaDock, Amber, OpenMM, GROMACS, NAMD, VMD, PyMOL, Chimera
  • Statistics: Markov State Models, Mutual Information Calculations
  • Simulation Techniques: All-atom simulations, Coarse Graining, Umbrella Sampling, Metadynamics, Free Energy Landscapes, Estimating Rate Constants
  • Machine Learning: Generative Neural Networks, Boltzmann Generators
  • Analysis Tools: cpptraj, MDAnalysis, mdtraj, RDKit

Research Experience

My research work includes activation studies of non-Class A G Protein-Coupled Receptors (GPCRs) using extensive molecular dynamics, information theory, and deep learning techniques. I have also explored design rules of protein mutations affecting protein complex formation. My work has been published in journals such as the Biophysical Journal and ACS Central Science.

Publications and Presentations

I have authored multiple research papers and have presented my work at various conferences, including the American Chemical Society Spring Meeting and the Annual Biophysical Society Meeting. My publications are available on platforms like Google Scholar and Biorxiv.
